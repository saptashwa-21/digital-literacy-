# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers,
      and symbols. Never reuse the same password across platforms.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts (email, social media,
      banking apps). Prefer app-based 2FA (like Google Authenticator) over SMS where possible.

- [ ] **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Software updates patch security vulnerabilities
      that cybercriminals actively exploit.

- [ ] **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.** Never log into banking apps, UPI, or
      email on unsecured public networks (cafes, college canteens, bus stands). Use a VPN if necessary.

- [ ] **Think before you click.** Do not open links or attachments in emails or WhatsApp messages
      from unknown senders — these are common phishing traps.

- [ ] **Review app permissions regularly.** Do not grant camera, microphone, or contacts access
      to apps that don't genuinely need them.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety (Specific Tips)

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank account details with anyone** — not even someone
      claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay, PhonePe, or NPCI support. Legitimate services
      will never ask for your PIN.

- [ ] **Beware of "receive money" scams on UPI.** Scammers send fake collect requests disguised
      as payment confirmations. Remember: entering your PIN always *sends* money — it never
      receives it. Always read the UPI prompt carefully before approving.

---

## Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Keep your social media profiles private.** Limit who can see your phone number, location,
      and personal photos. Oversharing makes you a target for identity theft and stalking.

- [ ] **Do not participate in viral quizzes or "personality tests"** that ask for personal details
      (birth date, mother's name, hometown) — these are often data harvesting tools.

---

## If Something Goes Wrong – Report It!

If you experience or witness any cybercrime, report it immediately:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (call from anywhere in India, 24x7) |
| **Your College IT Cell** | Report phishing emails targeting your institution |

> Reporting quickly increases the chance of recovering lost funds and prevents others from
> being victimised. You can report anonymously on the portal.
