# Task 3 – Reflection Notes
## Coding & Collaboration Platforms

---

### Part A – Coding Practice (CodeChef)

**Platform Used:** CodeChef (codechef.com)

I created my account on CodeChef and completed a beginner-level challenge
to get started with competitive programming. CodeChef is one of India's most
popular competitive coding platforms used by lakhs of students and
professionals to practice Data Structures, Algorithms, and problem solving.

The challenge I completed was a basic input/output problem where I had to
read two numbers and print their sum. Even though it sounds simple, it helped
me understand how online judges work — you write code, submit it, and the
platform automatically checks if your output is correct.

What I found interesting was the rating system on CodeChef. Every contest
you participate in affects your rating, just like a cricket ranking. It motivates
you to keep practicing regularly.

---

### Part B – Google Workspace Collaboration (Google Forms)

**Platform Used:** Google Forms + Google Sheets

I created a 5-question **Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz** using Google Forms.
The quiz includes:
- 4 Multiple Choice Questions covering topics like safe internet practices,
  digital literacy definition, cybersecurity, and professional platforms
- 1 Short Answer Question asking students about their personal online safety habit

Once the form was created, Google automatically linked it to a **Google Sheet**
that collects all responses in real time. This is extremely useful for teachers,
event organizers, or anyone who wants to collect and analyze data without
any coding knowledge.

---

### How These Tools Will Help Me Academically

Both CodeChef and Google Forms are tools I will use throughout my B.Tech:

- **CodeChef** will help me prepare for placement coding rounds, hackathons,
  and competitive programming contests. Starting from first year gives me a
  head start over students who begin in third year.

- **Google Forms** is useful for collecting peer feedback, conducting surveys
  for mini-projects, organizing events, and even submitting assignments in
  some courses. It is part of Google Workspace which is free for students.

Overall, this task made me realize that academic tools go far beyond just
attending classes — the right platforms can accelerate learning significantly.
